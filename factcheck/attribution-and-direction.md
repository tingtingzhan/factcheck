# Attribution & direction — who did what, to whom

Guards for assigning acts to the right party, in the right direction, at the right time.

>[!caution] Cause
>
>When assigning cause, put the focal party's own acts on the same ledger as counterparties' acts. Recoding a party's acts as purely "reactive/defensive" is permitted only with a cited hostile antecedent preceding each; absent that, count the act as a contributing cause, not an excused response.

>[!caution] Agency & Direction
>
>Before asserting who does/provides/refuses what, verify the **direction** against the record: which party is source, which is recipient. Don't infer direction from plausibility or a role label. Claims about who supplied, transferred, requested, or delivered what must name source and recipient and cite the passage.

>[!caution] Supply-Status Is the Provider's Act, Not the Recipient's Need
>
>Before asserting a provided good (support, access, cooperation, funding, protection, labor) is **still flowing** or **already cut**, fix its status from the **provider's** latest act on record, never from the **recipient's** continued need, dependence, or presence. Provision and its withdrawal are the provider's act; need is the recipient's state — the record can fix them in opposite directions at once. "Still relies on it / hasn't left" doesn't show it's still supplied; "no longer needs it" doesn't show it was withdrawn. Identify the provider first (→ *Agency & Direction*), then read that party's latest act.

>[!caution] Per-Item Attribution
>
>When a role, credit, status, or attribute is assigned item-by-item, verify it against the **specific item**. A role held on item A is not evidence of the same role on item B, even in a shared context. A **relational** attribute (a warm, hostile, or trusting tie) belongs to the *pair*, not the shared endpoint: that a party holds tie W with A is not evidence they hold W with B.

>[!caution] Aggregate Pattern → Single Party
>
>When the record states a *pattern* across several instances, verify **which party** owns **each** instance before attributing the count. A tally of N events across multiple actors is not evidence any one actor did N. Cite the passage fixing the actor per instance, not the summary.

>[!important] Broken-Commitment Verb
>
>Any verb implying a broken commitment ("renege," "back out," "abandon," "reverse," "foreclose," "held-until," "triggered-by") must specify *what* was reversed, owed *to whom*, and known *to whom*. A "held until X foreclosed it" claim must verify the foreclosing act *precedes* the conduct it allegedly foreclosed.

>[!caution] Interval & Temporal Anchor
>
>Before asserting one event happened "N units before/after" another, name **both** anchors and verify each is fixed on the record. Don't invent an anchor from an event the record only proposes or leaves open-ended ("as soon as possible"). Read ordering off the record's timestamps, not the more dramatic ordering — "X shortly before Y" and "Y shortly after X" are different claims. Attach the interval to the pair the record actually dates.

>[!caution] Read the Full Span
>
>Before treating a demand, obligation, or commitment as **one-off or bounded**, carry the reach the record fixes. When the wording extends it forward or open-endedly ("and the next," "ongoing," "thereafter"), a nearby deadline is one point inside the span, not its end. A bounded item may be dischargeable; the same item at open-ended reach is not — a backward-looking cure can't close a forward-reaching span.

>[!caution] Continuity ≠ Transition
>
>When a passage reports one thing **ending** alongside another **remaining**, that fixes two *states*, not a *transition* between them. Before asserting a party switched, migrated, or moved from A to B, cite the passage recording the **act**, not two adjacent facts. Aspect markers (*remains, still, continues, unchanged*) positively exclude a change; *becomes, switched to, replaced* mark one. A manufactured transition silently imports an actor and a motive, and inverts the fact's significance ("unaffected by the change in X" vs. "adjusted in response to X").

>[!caution] Distinguish Repeated Same-Type Events Before Attaching a Cause
>
>When the record has several events of the same kind (releases, filings, emails, meetings, versions), don't merge them into one "the X." Each carries distinguishing keys — date, channel, actor, target, version — so fix the specific instance before asserting anything; a property of one (motive, effect, trigger, audience) is not a property of its siblings. Then gate the cause to that instance's own timeline: a motive attaches only if its antecedent is on record *as of that instance's date*; explaining an earlier instance with facts true only later is backdating a cause. Channel/venue is load-bearing (private draft vs. public registry), not decoration.
